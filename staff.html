Dana Gepfrey, OTRL

Dana Gepfrey is the Executive Director (owner) and the Occupational Therapist. She has always dreamed of opening her own clinic and provide the best possible patient care. Dana has a passion for knowledge and will continue to research new treatment options for Autism and other pediatric conditions. Read More...

Dana has a Master’s degree in Occupational Therapy along with a Bachelor of Science degree in Counseling Psychology. She specializes in pediatric and hand therapy and has extensive experience with sensory integration, developmental, and handwriting treatments. She also has training in oral motor, feeding, and torticollis treatments. In the adult population, Dana has experience in orthopedic and neurological conditions involving the upper extremities and abilities to perform activities of daily living. In her free time, she enjoys being outdoors, camping, swimming, and trying new arts and crafts.

Allysha Walters, SLP

Allysha graduated from Central Michigan University with her bachelor’s in Communication Disorders and a minor in American Sign Language. She received her master’s degree from Eastern Illinois University in speech-language pathology. She is certified by the American Speech and Hearing Association (ASHA) and is a member of the Michigan Speech and Hearing Association (MSHA). Read More...

Allysha is experienced in both adult and pediatric speech therapy. She has received training with Pragmatic Organization Dynamic Display (PODD) and radiation safety in fluoroscopy. Allysha has worked with a varying degree of disorders/disabilities including articulation/phonological disorders, processing, cognition, augmentative and alternative communication, aphasia, apraxia, dysphagia, fluency and voice disorders. Allysha enjoys spending time with her huge family, friends and especially her cat Tipsy. In her free time she loves being outdoors, shopping and getting lost in a good mystery novel.

Rebecca is certified by the American Speech and Hearing Association, LSVT Loud certified, and trained in Language Acquisition through Motor Planning (LAMP). Read More...

She is experienced in both adult and pediatric speech therapy, with extensive work with autism, apraxia, and augmentative/alternative communication. She is certified in the Listening Program. Rebecca enjoys spending time with her husband and two children, as well as the family’s two dogs.
